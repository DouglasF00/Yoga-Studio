About Us

Our Story

In 1993, the doors to the small garage in Chapel Hill opened and the founder of Yoga Studio Chapel Hill, Samantha Smith, started her Yoga practice. Throughout the years, the studio has kept on expanding to what we know today with 5 yoga studios and 30 instructors. We embrace a warm and fufilling environment so you can step in, relax and keep your thought and feelings present from the very first second.

No matter what level or abilities you have, we have something for you. Our selection of classes varies in intensity and can be scaled and adjusted to your comfort and flexibility. Choose from our wide range of classes: Power Yoga, Restorstive Yoga, Hot Yoga, Yin Yoga, Pilates or Meditation. With a variety of over 35 yoga instructors, we have no doubt that you'll find your perfect match. If you have any question, feedback or concern, do not hesitate to reach out to us. We'd love to hear from you!

"It's not about being good at something. It's about being good to yourself."

- Samantha Smith

Our Purpose

We believe in the indiivuals ability to remain and rediscover it's own, true potential and strength. Well thought trough yoga classes will create flexibility and movement no matter age or background. To create a flow with recognition, the yoga positions and sequences can become meditation within movement.

We want to spread knowledge between the body and mind which leads to a healthy performance and practice as well as break out of bad habits and patterns and change out perspective. We encourage to be brave and playful during your practice to develop and live your full potential. Let your personality, creativity, inspiration, fantasy and humour shine through!

Yoga is for your body and mind and we think highly of the benefits of our practice. In today's society, it's more needed than ever to have a safe place where you can gather your thoughts and feelings and get an outlet for them. By adding your breathwork and to use your body and mind as one, we're proud to contribute to a less stressed society and to decrease the risk of mental illness and other stress related consequences.
